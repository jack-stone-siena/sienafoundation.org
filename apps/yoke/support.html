Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data private?

Yes. All data — examination results, confession records, prayer sessions, patron saint dates — is stored exclusively on your device. Nothing leaves your phone. See our Privacy Policy.

What tradition does the examination of conscience follow?

The examination is structured according to the seven principal virtues and their opposing vices as laid out in the Summa Theologiae of St. Thomas Aquinas (ST II-II). The questions are gated by vocation and cover approximately 350 areas for lay persons and up to 546 for ordained.

What calendar does the Liturgical Calendar use?

The app uses the traditional Roman calendar (1962 Missal). Feast days for the Patron Saints feature are drawn from fixed feasts in this calendar.

What Bible translation is used?

The app uses the Douay-Rheims Bible, the standard English translation of the Vulgate, throughout — in Lectio Divina, the Psalter, and all Scripture references.

Can I delete my data?

Yes. All data is stored locally on your device. You can delete the app at any time to remove all associated data. Within the app, individual assessments and confession records can be deleted from the History screen.

The app is not working as expected. What should I do?

First try force-closing and reopening the app. If the issue persists, email us at apps@sienafoundation.org with a description of the problem and your iOS version. We will respond promptly.

I have a theological question about the content.

We welcome theological feedback. The app is built on Thomistic principles and we take doctrinal accuracy seriously. Email us at apps@sienafoundation.org with your question or concern.
